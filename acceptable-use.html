Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: 16 June 2026

Nudge is a coaching tool meant to help you communicate better and more genuinely. This policy sets out what you may not do with it. Breaking these rules can result in suspension or removal of your account.

You must not use Nudge to:

  • Upload content involving anyone under 18, or content that sexualizes minors in any way.
  • Harass, threaten, stalk, intimidate, or bully any person.
  • Deceive, defraud, catfish, or impersonate someone, or manipulate a person into actions against their interest.
  • Coerce, pressure, or undermine another person's ability to freely consent.
  • Upload other people's private conversations or images where you don't have the right to share them.
  • Generate or seek sexually explicit, hateful, violent, or otherwise abusive content.
  • Break any applicable law, or violate the rights (including privacy) of others.
  • Attempt to reverse-engineer, overload, scrape, or circumvent the security or access controls of the service.

Use it in good faith

Nudge is designed to help you show up as a more confident, thoughtful version of yourself — not to manipulate or mislead anyone. Suggestions are aids, not scripts; the responsibility for how you communicate, and for treating others with respect, is yours.

Reporting abuse

If you believe someone is misusing Nudge, or you have a safety concern, contact support@trynudge.com.

Enforcement

We may review accounts and remove access where we reasonably believe this policy has been breached, with or without notice depending on the severity.

This is a starter template provided for convenience, not legal advice. An acceptable-use policy is especially important for an AI product — have a professional review it before scaling.
